The Wall is the eleventh studio album by Pink Floyd, released on 30 November 1979 by Harvest/EMI and Columbia/CBS Records. It is a rock opera which explores Pink, a jaded rock star, as he constructs a psychological "wall" of social isolation. Pink Floyd's "The Wall" album is primarily categorized as progressive rock, specifically a subgenre of progressive rock art rock. It also incorporates elements of psychedelic rock and concept album. While it's often labeled as progressive rock, some argue it leans more towards art rock, particularly due to its theatrical and narrative approach.
